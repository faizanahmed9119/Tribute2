Muhammad Islam Gill

An eminent cotton scientist of Pakistan

Here's a timeline of M.Islam Gill's life:

  • 1947 - Born in Faisalabad, Pakistan
  • 1950 to 64 - Got his primary and higher education from Faisalabad
  • 1968 - Graduated from University of Agriculture Faisalabad with BSC Honors Agriculture
  • 1970 - Graduated from University of Agriculture Faisalabad with Masters in Soil Science
  • 1970 to 72 - Worked in Soil Chemistry Department at Ayub Agriculture Research Insitute
  • 1972 - Joined Central Cotton Research Institute Multan(CCRI) as a Scientific Officer
  • 1975 - Travelled to California, USA for further studies in Agriculture
  • 1976 - Promoted to Senior Scientific Officer and Head of Agronomy Section at CCRI Multan
  • 1983 - Awarded a scholarship for further studies at a university in Melbourne, Austrailia
  • 1986 - Introduced the "Bed & Furrow" method for the cultivation of cotton in Pakistan
  • 1991 - Promoted to Pricipal Scientific Officer at CCRI Multan
  • 2002 - Appointed Director Central Cotton Research Institute Multan(CCRI)
  • 2006 - Awarded "Tamgha-E-Imtiaz" by President of Pakistan for his excellent contributions in the field of cotton research
  • 2006 - Passed away in a tragic plane crash

Contributions to the research and development of cotton in Pakistan:

  • Development of low cost cotton production technology and weed management
  • Use of chemical control of weeds through pre-emergence herbicides in cotton which was exclaimed not only at the national but also at the international level
  • Innovative improvement in farm machinery for line-sowing and inter-culturing
  • Bed-furrow planting technology economiting the water requirements by 30%
  • During his four years tenure as the Director, CCRI, Multan, five high yielding cotton varieties (GIM-496, CIM-473, CIM-505, CIM-499, CIM-707) were reisased and benefited the growing community and the textile industry
  • He was growers friendly and made untiring efforts to solve the small growers problems, improve their yield and profitability
  • He was also instrumental in promoting cultivation of cotton in Balochistan and NWFP and took practical steps for the technology transfer to the cotton growers
  • He promoted the adoption of integrated pest management (IPM) throughout the country with visible success
  • Under his guidance, the CCRI, Multan has achieved success in transfering the virus resistant character from wild species into commercial upland cotton varieties
